# Figure Captions for Paper Draft

**Figure 1. Reframed study model.** The experiment is positioned as an evaluation of an instrumented immersive training platform. The VR task produces objective performance logs and subjective experience measures, which support learning, feasibility, and future stress-validation claims.

**Figure 2. Procedural efficiency across chronological runs.** Individual participant trajectories, group means, and 95% confidence intervals for total completion time. The Friedman test indicates a significant repeated-exposure effect.

**Figure 3. Interaction errors across chronological runs.** Individual trajectories, group means, and 95% confidence intervals for false-grab counts. The direction suggests stabilization, but the omnibus test should be interpreted conservatively.

**Figure 4. Objective outcomes by randomized scene condition.** Mean +/- 95% CI for total time and false grabs in Guided, Autonomous, and Stress conditions. Stress is descriptively harder, but condition effects are not statistically confirmed in the current sample.

**Figure 5. Feasibility thresholds.** Participant-level averages across sessions for acceptance, presence/realism, and VR symptoms. Threshold tests show high acceptance and presence with low symptoms.

**Figure 6. Evidence map.** Summary of proposed hypotheses, endpoints, p-values, effect sizes, and recommended claim strength for an ICINCO-oriented submission.
