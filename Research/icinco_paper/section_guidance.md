# Section Guidance for the ICINCO Draft

## Overall Reviewer Strategy

Position the paper as a pilot evaluation of an instrumented immersive training platform, not as a definitive proof that one condition is superior. ICINCO/INSTICC reviewers explicitly evaluate relevance, originality, technical quality, significance, presentation, experimental results, comparative evaluation, figures, critical discussion and conclusion strength. The draft is therefore structured to make the strongest defensible claim first: the system produces measurable behavioral evidence of procedural learning and is feasible for repeated use.

## Title, Abstract and Keywords

Guidance: Make the technical contribution visible immediately. Avoid a title that sounds like a generic VR satisfaction study. The abstract must stay within the SCITEPRESS limit of 70-200 words.

Current choice: The title foregrounds instrumented immersive digital-twin training, smart-factory procedural learning and pilot evaluation. The abstract reports the system, protocol, objective metrics, key p-values and limitations.

## Introduction

Guidance: Establish the practical problem, the research gap and the contribution. Avoid overclaiming. Reviewers need to know why the work matters beyond building a VR scene.

Current logic: Smart factories need measurable procedural training. VR is useful only if instrumented. The contribution is therefore a measurement-capable VR task and pilot evaluation.

## Related Work

Guidance: Use a compact but credible set of references. For a short conference paper, related work should support the framing, not become a thesis chapter.

Current logic: Three clusters are used: digital twins/smart factories, immersive VR for industrial learning, and guidance/autonomy/presence measurement.

## System Design

Guidance: Make originality and technical quality visible. Explain what the platform logs and why that matters.

Current logic: The section describes the broader digital-twin project context, the nine-step VR labeling task, the guidance variants and objective logging. Figure 1 shows representative environment states.

## Experimental Protocol

Guidance: Be transparent and reproducible. Explain the 12-participant sample, within-subject structure, randomized but imbalanced realized order, metrics and analysis. Do not hide the pilot nature.

Current logic: The protocol separates chronological run effects from scene-condition effects. This protects the paper from a common reviewer criticism: confusing learning order with condition effects.

## Results

Guidance: Put the strongest result first. Report means, SDs, p-values and effect sizes. Clearly label partial and non-significant results.

Current logic: Completion time is primary. False grabs are secondary. Feasibility thresholds are strong. Stress condition and presence-performance correlation are exploratory.

## Discussion

Guidance: Explain what the results mean and what they do not prove. A strong discussion increases reviewer trust.

Current logic: The paper claims measurable learning and feasibility. It does not claim definitive stress-condition superiority or presence-performance prediction.

## Threats to Validity

Guidance: Directly name weaknesses before reviewers do: sample size, demographic imbalance, realized order imbalance, no baseline group, five step-time mismatches, missing stress-event severity logs and ceiling effects.

Current logic: The threats section is intentionally explicit and academically conservative.

## Conclusion

Guidance: End with the central contribution and realistic next steps.

Current logic: The conclusion emphasizes the instrumented platform, learning signal and future work with larger samples, baseline comparisons, event logs and retention tests.
